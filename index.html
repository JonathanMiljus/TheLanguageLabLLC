About

Researcher. Practitioner.
Lifelong Language Learner.

I'm Jonathan Miljus — an applied linguist, multilingual educator, and SLA researcher based in Southwest Florida. Through The Language Laboratory LLC, I provide one-on-one and small-group academic English consultation to international students, doctoral candidates, and professionals navigating English-medium academic and professional environments.

I began studying Latin and German at a young age, and for years I dreamed of becoming a Germanist and academic linguist. Life took me on a different path — through careers in business, healthcare, and eventually into teaching — but languages remained at the center of everything. At 33, I acquired Spanish through immersion with my wife's family, and today I use it daily alongside French and English in my multilingual classroom.

That trajectory — from classical languages as a kid to adult Romance language acquisition in a cross-cultural family — gives me an unusually personal understanding of how languages are learned across a lifetime. It's not just theory for me. I've lived it.

Research & Frameworks

Where Theory Meets Practice

The Triple Input Cycle

Perception → Interaction → Autonomy

My original pedagogical framework for structuring content delivery in multilingual classrooms. It moves learners from comprehensible input (Perception) through scaffolded, collaborative meaning-making (Interaction) to independent production (Autonomy). This framework is the foundation of my doctoral research and daily classroom practice.

Doctoral Research

Investigating the intersection of translanguaging, AI-assisted instruction, and content-area literacy for multilingual learners. My goal is to understand how technology and students' full linguistic repertoires can be leveraged together to deepen academic achievement.

"Bridging the Linguistic Gap"

My doctoral writing sample exploring research-based instructional approaches to multilingual learner achievement in content-area classrooms — examining how sheltered instruction models and translanguaging pedagogy can work in tandem.
